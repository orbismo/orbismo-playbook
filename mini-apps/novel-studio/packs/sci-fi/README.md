# Sci-Fi (genre pack)

The genre's rigor as trackable machinery, for a [Novel Studio](../../) novel. It adds two thread types to the studio's promise/payoff ledger — `technology` (introduced with stated constraints, owing the reader consequences: the constraint bites or the capability changes something visible) and `anomaly` (the mystery-of-the-world, owing an explanation or an honest, noted silence) — and four review lenses: **Tech Consistency** (nothing outruns its stated limits), **Chekhov's Tech** (no established capability sits unfired through a crisis it would trivially solve), **Extrapolation Integrity** (the world shows its inventions' second-order effects), and the **Timeline Check** (story time survives relativity, cryosleep, and comm lag).

The lenses lean on the core's **canon rules**: your world's constraints (how fast the drive goes, what the AI may not do) recorded as `rule` entities with `rule_type: "canon"` and listed in the novel's index. Say "in this world, FTL takes a week per light-year" and the studio records it; Tech Consistency then enforces it. A novel with no canon rules still works — those lenses report "no canon rules recorded" once and move on.

Requires the Novel Studio app. Install by giving [pack.json](pack.json) to your connected AI; then say "adopt the Sci-Fi pack for \<novel\>" (recorded in that novel's Blueprint).

Packs are data, not rules — this installs one `reference` entity (`reference/genre_pack_sci_fi`) and touches nothing else.
